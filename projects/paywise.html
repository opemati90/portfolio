01 Framing the Problem

5W1H: Defining the design challenge

Who

Young adults (18-35), freelancers, and small business owners managing multiple cards and accounts

What

A unified mobile app for card management, expense tracking, budgeting, and savings goals

When

Daily financial decisions: spending, saving, budgeting, and tracking across multiple payment methods

Where

Mobile-first, used at point of purchase, during commutes, and during monthly budget reviews

Why

68% of users in our survey do not track expenses. Existing tools are fragmented across 5+ apps

How

Auto-categorized transactions, visual budgets, safe-to-spend indicators, and a digital card wallet

02 Research

Survey (n=19) + interviews (n=6)

I ran a quantitative survey covering demographics, expense tracking habits, and feature priorities. Followed with 6 in-depth interviews to understand the emotional relationship with money management.

Key findings

68.4% do not track expenses. Primary reason: "too much time." 42.1% aged 18-25. Employment split evenly across employed, unemployed, and self-employed.

Interview insights

Users feel guilt about spending but lack visibility. They want to know "am I on track this month?" at a glance, not after 10 minutes of spreadsheet work.

04 Final Designs

One app, complete financial picture

The home screen shows total balance, income vs. expenses, and a "safe to spend" indicator. Transactions auto-categorize. Budgets show progress visually. Goals track savings with clear timelines.

Validation

"Safe to spend" was the most loved feature

9/12 users said it changed how they think about money. It replaces anxiety with a concrete, instant answer.

Improvement

Transaction filter needed presets

Users wanted quick filters like "This week" and "Food only" instead of building custom filters from scratch.

06 Reflections

What I learned

Financial apps are emotional products

The "safe to spend" feature worked because it replaced anxiety with a concrete answer. Design for the emotion, not just the data.

Auto-categorization is table stakes

No one wants to tag every coffee purchase. The moment we automated categories, daily engagement jumped. Reduce effort, increase value.
