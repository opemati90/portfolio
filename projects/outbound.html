01 Framing the Problem

5W1H: Defining the design challenge

Who

Frequent travelers aged 22-38 who plan trips on the go and want personalized experiences

What

An AI-powered app that creates complete itineraries from a single prompt, with booking and tracking

When

As AI makes personalized trip planning possible in seconds rather than hours of manual research

Where

Mobile-first (iOS), used during inspiration, planning, booking, and travel phases

Why

67% of travel app users abandon onboarding if it takes more than 45 seconds. No competitor shows value before sign-up

How

AI itinerary generation, browse-before-signup pattern, passport gamification for retention, and shared trip collaboration

03 Final Designs

AI-powered, warm, adventure-ready

The orange-and-white palette evokes warmth and adventure. AI generates complete itineraries from natural language prompts. The passport feature tracks trips and collects continent stamps, creating engagement beyond trip planning.

Validation

Passport feature created genuine delight

7/8 users smiled or said "cool" when reaching the Passport screen. Emotional design works.

Critical

OTP error message too generic

"Invalid code" was not helpful. Changed to "Code does not match, check your email" with a resend timer.

05 Reflections

What I learned

Emotional milestones in onboarding

The "Passport Approved" moment was the single most commented-on decision. Onboarding should not just be functional. It should make users feel something.

Error states are conversations

"Oops!" is not helpful. "The code does not match, we just sent a new one" is. Every error state is a conversation with an anxious user.
